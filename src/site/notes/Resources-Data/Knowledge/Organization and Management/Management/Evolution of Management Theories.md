Management theories developed over time as organizations became ==more complex==. These theories provide different perspectives on how organizations should be managed.

## A. Classical Management Theory

+---------------------------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| 1. **Scientific Management** (***Fredrick W. Taylor***) | * Focuses on ==improving efficiency== through scientific study of work methods.                           |
|                                                         | * Emphasizes **standardization**, **time and motion studies**, and **worker productivity**.               |
+---------------------------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| 2. **Administrative Management** (***Henri Fayol***)    | * Focuses on ==management principles== such as **division of work**, **unity of command**, and authority. |
|                                                         | * Introduced the ==five functions of management==.                                                        |
+---------------------------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| 3. **Bureaucratic Management** (***Max Weber***)        | * Emphasizes **formal rules**, **hierarchy**, and **clear division of labor**.                            |
+---------------------------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+

## B. Behavioral Management Theory
* Focuses on **human behavior**, **motivation**, and **leadership**.
* Highlights the importance of ==employee satisfaction and teamwork==.
* Key contributors include ***Elton Mayo*** and the ***Hawthrone Studies***.

## C. Quantitative Management Theory
* Uses ==mathematical models and statistical techniques== to aid decision-making.
* Commonly applied in **operations** and **production management**.

## D. Modern Management Theories

+---------------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| 1. **Systems Theory**     | Views the organization as a system composed of ==interrelated parts==.                          |
+---------------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
| 2. **Contingency Theory** | Suggests that there is no one best way to manage, management practices depend on the situation. |
+---------------------------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+

## Pre-Colonial Period (*Before 1521*)
1. **Art** - integrated into ==daily rife and rituals== — used for **religious**, **social**, and **practical purposes**.
2. Early **art** forms include **pottery**, **weaving**, **sculpture**, **tattooing**, and **musical instruments**.
3. **Pre-colonial art** - **art** ==before foreign colonization==.
4. **Functional art** - serves a ==practical purpose== (e.g. **jars**, **mats**, **traps**, **ornaments**).
5. **Ritual art** - used in ==spiritual practices and ceremonies==.
6. **Kanyaw** - **Cordillera** ritual with ==animal sacrifice== and divination officiated by a shaman.
7. **Pagdiwata** - ritual by the ***Tagbanwa*** people for ==thanksgiving and harvest==.
8. **Tagbanwa Belief** - ==Every 13th moon==, three goddesses descend to ==bless rice planting==.
9. **Manunggul Jar** (***Palawan***) - secondary burial jar symbolizing ==life after death==.
10. **Boxer Codex** - ancient manuscript depicting early Filipinos' **attire**, **tattoos**, and **jewelry**.
11. **Kendi** - Round-bodied, ==handle-less vessel== for liquids.
12. **Bulul** - ==Wooden rice guardian== figure of the ***Ifugao***.
13. **Baybayin** - Pre-colonial scripts inscribed on **bamboo** or **leaves**.
14. **Islas de los Pintados** - "*Islands of the Painted*" (refers to tattooed ***Visayans***).
15. **Kulintang** - Set of **bossed gongs** from ***Mindanao***.
16. **Kudyapi** - Two-stringed lute.
17. **Bungkaka** - Bamboo buzzer.
18. **Litguit** / **Faglong** - Stringed instruments used by ==indigenous groups==.
19. **Inabel** - Textile from ***Ilocos***.
20. **Tepo** - ==Mat woven== from **pandan leaves** (***Sama*** people).
21. **Bubo** - ==Fish trap== made of **woven bamboo** (***Ilocos***).
22. Woodcarving towns:
	* ***Paete***, ***Laguna***
	* ***Betis***, ***Pampanga***
23. **Okir** - ***Maranao*** ornamental design (==geometric & floral==).
24. **Panolong** - ==Carved beam== on **Torogan** (**Maranao** royal house).
## Spanish Colonial Period (*1521-1898*)
* **Religious art** dominated — **churches**, **santos**, **retablos**, and **religious paintings**.
* Introduction of ==Western techniques and catholic iconography==.
* **Art** became an instrument for ==evangelization==.

1. **Baroque style** - grand, ornate, strong structures (for ==earthquakes and typhoons==).
2. **Folk / Tropical Baroque** - native adaption of **European Baroque**.
3. **Paoay Church** (***Ilocos Norte***) - famous for ==massive buttresses==.
4. **Miag-ao Church** - example of **Baroque** architecture with ==native motifs==.
5. **Retablo** - ==altar niche== for **santos**.
6. **Relleves** - carved figures protruding from ==walls or altars==.
7. **Plateria** - hammered silver decorations.
8. **Pieta** - sculpture of ***Mary*** holding ***Jesus***' dead body.
9. ***Doctrina Christiana*** (***1953***) - ==first printed book==; contained ==Christian teachings==.
10. **Xylography** - woodcut printing technique used for ***Doctrina Christiana***.
11. ***Carta Hydrographica y Chorographica*** (***1734***) - first scientific map.
12. **Pasyon** - chant narrating ***Christ***'s passion.
13. **Awit and Corrido** - narrative poems about ==heroes and saints==; based on ==European tales==.
14. **Kundiman** - evolved from ==love song to patriotic song==.
15. **Folk dances** showing ==cultural exchange==:
	1. **Balitao**
	2. **Polka**
	3. **Carinosa**
	4. **Pandanggo sa Ilaw**
16. **Komedya** - depicts battle between **Christians** and **Muslims**.
17. **Komedya de Santo** - Religious-themed **komedya**.
18. **Senakulo** - dramatization of ***Christ***'s passion.
19. **Sarsuwela** - musical play combining **song**, **dance**, and **dialogue**.
20. ***Atang de la Rama*** - queen of **Kundiman** and **National Artist for Theater and Music** (***1987***).
21. ***Gaspar Aquino de Belen*** - wrote the first passion play (*1704*).
22. ***Juan Luna*** - "***Spoliarium***," "***Espana y Filipinas***."
23. ***Felix Resurreccion Hidalgo*** - "*virgenes cristianas expuestas al populacho.*"
24. ***Rizal***'**s Toast** - "*Genius knows no country,*" honoring ***Luna*** and ***Hidalgo***.

## American Colonial Period (*1898 - 1940s*)
* Introduction of **secular** and **modern art**.
* Rise of ==nationalism and new art education== (***UP School of Fine Arts***).
* **Art** became a tool for ==education and nationalism==
1. ***Fernando Amorsolo*** - known for light-filled, ==romantic paintings of rural life== (e.g. ***Planting Rice***, ***Dalagang Filipina***).
2. ***Guillermo Tolentino*** - sculpted ***Oblation*** (***UP***) and ***Bonifacio Monument*** (***Caloocan***).
3. **Sedition Law** - banned plays and publications promoting ==independence==.
4. ***Juan Abad***'s "***Kahapon, Ngayon, at  Bukas***" - banned nationalist play.

## Japanese Period (*1941-1945*)
* **Art** and media were censored and used for ==propaganda==.
* Decline in **art** production due to **World War II**.
1. **KALIBAPI** (***Kapisanan sa Paglilingkod sa Bagong Pilipinas***) - controlled cultural activites.
2. **Hodobu** - japanese propaganda department.
3. **Music** - limited; focus shifted to ==traditional and folk songs==.

## Contemporary Period (*1946-Present*)
* National artists celebrated ==Filipino identity and modern themes==.
* Blend of ==traditional and modern techniques==.

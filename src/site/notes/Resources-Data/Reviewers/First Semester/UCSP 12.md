1. ***Homo habilis*** - known as the "==*handy man*==" species because they were the first to use ==simple stone stools==.
2. ***Homo erectus*** - referred to as the "*==working man==*", they were the first human species to ==control fire and cook food==.
3. ***Homo ergaster*** - ==developed advanced tools== and migrated widely across **Africa** and **Asia**.
4. ***Homo neanderthalensis*** - close to ==modern humans== and practiced ==burial rituals and symbolic thought==.
5. ***Homo sapiens*** - known as ==modern humans==, they developed **language**, **art**, and **complex culture**.
6. **Paleolithic Period** - characterized by ==crude stone tools and a hunting-gathering lifestyle==.
7. **Mesolithic Period** - where **microliths** (==small stone tools==) and semi-permanent settlements appeared.
8. **Neolithic Period** - marked by the ==development of agriculture and domestication of animals==.
9. **Barangay** - ==early organized community== in the ***Philippines*** led by a **datu**.
10. "**I**" - The **spontaneous**, **creative,** and **unpredictable** part of the self.
11. "**Me**" - The **socialized**, **rule-following**, and **organized** aspect of the self.
12. **Socialization** - process by which individuals learn and internalize **societal norms**, **values**, and **behaviors** to function in society.
13. **Conformity** - adjusting one's behavior to ==match group norms==.
14. **Obedience** - ==following an order== given by a person in authority.
15. **Social Control** - the mechanisms that regulate ==individual and group behavior==.
	1. **Informal Social Control** - uses **shame**, **gossip**, **praise**, and **family influence**.
	2. **Formal Social Control** - uses **laws**, **rules**, and **punishments**.
16. **Deviance** - behavior that ==violates social norms==.
	1. **Positive Deviance** - overconformity to norms that ==benefits society==.
	2. **Negative Deviance** - violations that ==harm or disrupt norms==.
17. ***Emile Durkheim*** - explained that **deviance** helps ==clarify moral boundaries and promotes social order==.
18. **Conflict Theory** - states that **deviance** occurs because **laws** ==favor the powerful and disadvantage the poor==.
19. **Symbolic Interactionism** - explains that **deviance** is learned and labeled through ==social interaction==.
20. **Common Good** - ==promotes the well-being== of all members of society.
21. **Human Rights** - basic entitlements such as **life**, **freedom**, and **education**.
22. **Human Dignity** - treating others with ==respect and equality== regardless of status.
23. **Equality** - ensuring that every individual is treated with ==fairness and justice==.
24. **Self** - shaped by ==interaction and social reflection==.

## Socialization
### Agents
1. **Family** - the ==primary agent==; teaches **moral values**, **customs**, and early **social skills**.
2. **School** - develops **discipline**, **cooperation**, and prepares individuals for **civic life**.
3. **Peer Group** - influences ==social behavior and group belonging==.
4. **Mass Media** - spreads **culture**, **information**, and **technology**.
5. **Religion** - provides ==moral and spiritual guidance==.
6. **Government** - enforces **laws** and maintains **social order**.
7. **Workplace** - prepares people for ==professional and adult roles==

### Functions
* Shapes ==personality and identity==
* Teaches ==cultural norms and societal expectations
* Builds ==relationships and social integration==
* Does not isolate individuals but ==connects them to society==.
